Sedari

The Jalabiyah is accompanied with multiple pieces, one of them is the Vest, known as the Sedari. It is a waistcoat/Vest that adds a sense of formality, elegance and modesty to the Jalabiyah. The Sedari is a sleeveless piece mad out of wool contains pockets, a maximum of 3 botton, as that is critical, and embroidery with multiple designs and eyelets which allows a belt for support during labor. It represents history, dating back to the days of the Kingdom of Kush around 2500 BC where the earliest structured Nubian dress was first reviled. Through Ottoman empire, it also dates to the Beja tribe which is mainly located in the eastern part of Sudan. The what was thought to be a slight pivotal wave of style, caught by the arab world. After a peace agreement between the Kingdom of kush, the Egyptians, and the Arabs, allowing migration into northern Sudan the Bedouin tribe, which is an Arab tribe, began to marry into the Nubians and Adapt the culture as well as the modesty if the Nubians. With the peace agreement and the ability for the Bedouin tribe to travel back and forth through Egypt and Palestine allowed the Serdari and its unique design to spread into the Arab peninsula where it was redesign to fit climate needs.
